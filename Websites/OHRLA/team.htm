About Dr. Vincent Biron:

Dr. Biron is an Assistant Professor and Surgeon-Scientist at the University of Alberta in the Department of Surgery, Division of Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery. He received his PhD in Medical Genetics at the University of Alberta in 2007 and a Doctor of Medicine degree from the University of Calgary in 2008. He completed a residency in Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery from the University of Alberta in 2013 and went on to obtain a fellowship in Head and Neck Oncologic Surgery from the University of California Davis in 2014. Dr. Biron has a large clinical practice focused on head and neck oncologic, transoral robotic and thyroid surgery. His research is focused on molecular biomarkers and epigenetics of head and neck cancer.

About Dr. Morris Kostiuk:

Dr. Kostiuk received his PhD in Cell Biology from the University of Alberta in 2009 and is currently a research associate with the Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery Research Laboratory of Alberta. He has contributed to 8 peer reviewed publications and 1 book chapter encompassing the fields of transcription, epigenetics, enzymology, post-translational protein modification, cancer and human-viral interactions.

About Cameron Lindsay:

Cameron Lindsay received his Master of Science in Experimental Surgery in 2018 from the University of Alberta under Dr. Vincent Biron. Cameron is currently continuing his position in the Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery Research Laboratory of Alberta as a research assistant.
